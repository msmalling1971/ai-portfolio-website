Case Study - AI Infrastructure

Building a Local AI Infrastructure Platform

A completed local AI platform build focused on GPU acceleration, recoverable infrastructure, and the operating discipline required to move from a lab experiment to a dependable platform baseline.

Objective

Build a controlled local AI platform that could host modern models and support future RAG, agent, and documentation workflows.

Purpose

The goal was not simply to run models. The platform needed to create repeatable AI infrastructure practice: hardware validation, model serving, local data control, recovery planning, and documentation that could support future platform engineering work.

Outcome

The final environment successfully hosted and executed Qwen 2.5 7B, Qwen 3 14B, and GPT-OSS 20B with GPU acceleration while adding snapshots, NAS backups, and a documented recoverable baseline.

Architecture

The platform connects physical GPU capacity to a user-facing AI workspace through a validated runtime path.

Logical Path
  1. Proxmox host
  2. RTX 3090 GPU
  3. NVIDIA driver stack
  4. CUDA runtime
  5. LXC AI container
  6. Open WebUI
  7. Ollama
  8. Large language models

Troubleshooting

The central engineering problem was not model selection. It was proving the platform could be trusted.

Challenge

Unstable GPU-accelerated workload behavior

Early testing produced model startup failures, slow responses, inconsistent GPU utilization, Ollama runner failures, and unexpected inference behavior.

Root Cause

GPU ownership conflict

Investigation showed the RTX 3090 was being claimed by a conflicting kernel-level component involving NovaCore, preventing the NVIDIA driver from attaching cleanly.

Decision

Rebuild from a trusted baseline

Once the environment had accumulated too much uncertainty, rebuilding became more effective than continuing to troubleshoot against a drifting platform state.

Resolution

A clean platform rebuild converted an unstable experiment into a recoverable AI infrastructure baseline.

  • 01Fresh Proxmox deployment and updated host platform
  • 02Removal of conflicting GPU ownership and NVIDIA driver validation
  • 03CUDA validation and GPU visibility verification
  • 04Open WebUI, Ollama, and model deployment testing
  • 05Snapshots, NAS backups, and GitHub documentation updates

Business Value

The project demonstrates AI infrastructure as infrastructure: architecture, reliability, recovery, documentation, and operational judgment.

Infrastructure Operations

Linux administration, platform troubleshooting, environment validation, and baseline management.

GPU Infrastructure

NVIDIA driver management, CUDA runtime validation, GPU workload execution, and ownership diagnosis.

Platform Engineering

Repeatable deployment practices, versioned documentation, operational procedures, and recoverability.

Next Iteration

Future work extends the platform toward benchmarking, RAG, vector databases, AI agents, and Kubernetes-based AI infrastructure.
